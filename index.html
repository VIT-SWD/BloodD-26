Before Donating – Safety Precautions

  • Be at least 18 years old
  • Hemoglobin should be 12.5 or above
  • Weight: Girls > 45kg | Boys > 50kg
  • Sleep well the night before
  • Eat a healthy meal before donation
  • Maintain 3 months gap between donations
  • Do not donate if sick or on medication
  • No alcohol within last 24 hours
  • Female donors should avoid during menstruation
  • Stay hydrated
  • Safety first – for you and the patient

Tips to Increase Hemoglobin

Iron Rich Foods
  • Spinach & green leafy vegetables
  • Beetroot & pomegranate
  • Dates & jaggery
  • Lentils and beans
Healthy Habits
  • Vitamin C (orange, lemon, amla) for absorption
  • Avoid tea/coffee after meals
  • Regular exercise
  • Proper sleep & hydration

A collection of frequently asked questions

  • I am scared of syringes.

    That's completely normal. The needle is sterile and used only once, and the donation takes just 3–5 minutes. A small moment of courage can save a life.

  • What if I faint?

    You are medically screened beforehand, and professionals are present throughout. If you feel dizzy, you'll be assisted immediately. Fainting is rare and temporary.

  • Does blood donation hurt?

    Just a small pinch for a few seconds. The happiness of helping someone lasts much longer.

  • How much blood is taken?

    Only 350–450 ml, which your body naturally replenishes in a few weeks.

  • How long does it take?

    Around 20–30 minutes in total; the actual donation takes just 3–5 minutes.

  • Why should you donate?

    Because your few minutes can give someone a second chance at life.
